In 2018, America has two remaining pastimes
Netflix
and memes
Here's how many people searched for those terms on Google for each day in late 2018
There's a clear cycle:
A couple days of Netflix on top
Then a few days of "Meme" taking over
And then back to Netflix, and so on
If we shade in weekends (Fri-Sat), the pattern becomes obvious
We watch Netflix on the weekend, and spend our weeks looking at memes
But in December, this trend changed, and Netflix dominated the search trends for the end of the year
What happened?
Netflix started streaming Bird Box on December 21, and searches for it immediately began climbing
Thanks in part to PR-friendly memes like the Bird Box challenge, Netflix boosted Bird Box to over 45 million views in a week

In return, Bird Box helped push the number of searches for Netflix to over double the volume for "meme" from December 26-29.

At the peak of its popularity (Dec. 27-29), searches for Bird Box even surpassed searches for Netflix itself.

The wild success of this meme-friendly marketing effort probably means we can expect to see many more meme-driven movies and PR campaigns in the future.
